# Toddler Tape Logo And Landing Image Brief

## Brand Snapshot

Toddler Tape is a pre-launch sensory play product: colorful dissolvable tape strips toddlers can tear, taste, and dissolve during supervised play. The brand should feel playful, clever, tactile, and parent-trustworthy, not chaotic, medical, candy-like, or generic toy-store cute.

Core promise: **The tape toddlers can eat.**

Primary audience: parents and caregivers of mouthy toddlers, especially during high-chair waits, travel, restaurant waits, and sibling craft time.

## Visual Thesis

Warm cream surfaces, chunky rounded type, coral-led accents, thick charcoal outlines, real product texture, and a few confident tape-tear details. The system should feel like a premium toddler product with a mischievous wink.

## Brand System

### Palette

- Cream: `#FFFBF5` for primary background.
- Charcoal: `#1A1A1A` for type, outlines, and high-trust contrast.
- Coral: `#E8735A` / `#FF6B47` for the primary logo accent, CTA, and hero product emphasis.
- Teal: `#5BBFB5` for dissolve/wetness/safety support.
- Sunshine: `#EDD97A` / `#F7DC6F` for taste, joy, and ratings.
- Lavender: `#C4A8D4` / `#B19CD9` for soft secondary play moments.
- Mint: `#7ECFB3` for plant-based/safety cues.

Use coral as the brand anchor. Use the other colors as product-pack and section accents, not as equal competing brand colors.

### Type

- Primary type direction: rounded, bold, friendly sans similar to Nunito Black.
- Secondary type direction: small monospace labels similar to JetBrains Mono for safety notes, section eyebrows, and microcopy.
- Avoid thin, elegant, sharp, handwritten, or childish alphabet-block typography.

### Illustration And Image Style

- Thick charcoal outline on product illustrations.
- Rounded, tactile geometry.
- Slightly imperfect torn tape edges.
- Warm daylight or clean studio lighting.
- Product should look edible-adjacent but not like candy, fruit roll-ups, medical tape, painter's tape, or adhesive bandages.
- Prefer cropped toddler hands, parent hands, trays, tables, and product moments over full child faces.
- If using photography, keep scenes clean, real, supervised, and product-first.

## Logo Brief

### Required Logo Assets

1. Primary horizontal logo: tape-roll mark plus `ToddlerTape` wordmark.
2. Stacked logo: mark above or beside two-line wordmark for square placements.
3. Icon mark: standalone tape roll with one small torn strip or bite/torn-edge cue.
4. One-color logo: charcoal on cream and cream on charcoal.
5. Favicon/social avatar: simplified mark readable at 32px.

### Logo Concept Direction

The mark should combine three ideas:

- A tape roll.
- A torn strip edge.
- A safe mouth-contact/taste cue.

Best direction: a coral tape roll with a cream center, thick charcoal outline, and a short torn strip peeling from the roll. Add a tiny bite-like notch or crumb detail only if it remains subtle and readable. The logo should still read as tape first.

Wordmark direction:

- `Toddler` in charcoal.
- `Tape` in coral.
- Rounded heavy sans, tight but not cramped.
- Optional custom detail: a torn notch, bite, or lifted corner in the `T` of `Tape`.

Avoid:

- Pacifiers, bottles, cartoon babies, mascots, teeth, mouths, tongues, or faces.
- Medical tape crosses or bandage shapes.
- Candy-wrapper styling.
- Overly wobbly letters that reduce trust.
- Any mark that implies the product is a snack or food substitute.

## Product Pack Specifics

### Rainbow Rips

Mood: bright, celebratory, first-pack favorite.

Colors: coral `#E8735A`, yellow `#F7DC6F`, green `#77DD77`, teal `#4ECDC4`, lavender `#B19CD9`, pink `#FF9AA2`.

Pattern: wide, chunky stripes with uneven torn edges.

### Dino Dots

Mood: earthy, playful, prehistoric without becoming mascot-heavy.

Colors: olive `#556B2F`, sage `#8FBC8F`, terracotta `#D2691E`, tan `#DEB887`, green `#6B8E23`, sand `#F4A460`.

Pattern: large soft dots. Optional tiny fossil/footprint detail as a secondary texture only.

### Berry Stripes

Mood: sweet, bold, berry-toned, but not candy.

Colors: magenta `#C71585`, rose `#DB7093`, light pink `#FFB6C1`, plum `#8B008B`, orchid `#DA70D6`, hot pink `#FF69B4`.

Pattern: playful stripes with high contrast.

### Sunny Shapes

Mood: warm, sunny, optimistic.

Colors: gold `#FFD700`, orange `#FFA500`, deep orange `#FF8C00`, pale yellow `#FFEC8B`, khaki `#F0E68C`, light peach `#FFE4B5`.

Pattern: simple circles, squares, triangles, and wavy blocks.

## Safety And Claim Guardrails

Use these claims:

- Designed for supervised sensory play.
- Mouth-contact in mind.
- Quick-dissolve format.
- No pressure-sensitive sticky adhesive.
- Holds by texture, not chemical bond.
- Starter packs coming soon.

Do not use these claims unless legally/product-approved:

- 100% food-grade.
- Certified safe.
- Allergy-free.
- Medical, therapeutic, or developmental claims.
- Product is food, snack, candy, supplement, or medicine.

Every safety visual should feel transparent and responsible. Trust comes from clarity and restraint, not from overpromising.

## Designer Checklist

- Product reads as tape before it reads as edible.
- Logo is readable at nav size and favicon size.
- Coral remains the dominant brand accent.
- Product pack colors are distinct but belong to one system.
- Every image has one focal point.
- No image makes the product look unsupervised, sticky, medical, or candy-like.
- Safety visuals avoid fake certifications and overclaiming.
- Hero and logo should still feel premium if all decorative motion is removed.
